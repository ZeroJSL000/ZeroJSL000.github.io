Q2 Survival Analysis Report

1. Dataset and Task

This analysis uses the IBM Telco Customer Churn dataset. Each row represents one customer. The goal is to analyze how long customers stay with the company and what factors may affect customer churn risk.

2. Survival Analysis Setup

In this case, the duration variable is tenure, which measures the number of months a customer has stayed with the company. The event variable is Churn. If Churn is Yes, event is coded as 1. If Churn is No, event is coded as 0, meaning the observation is right-censored.

3. Data Cleaning

The analysis keeps month-to-month customers and removes customers without internet service. After cleaning, the dataset contains 3351 customers, including 1556 churned customers and 1795 censored customers.

4. Exploratory Results

The cleaned sample has a churn rate of about 46.43%. Fiber optic customers show a higher churn rate than DSL customers. Customers using Electronic check also show a higher churn rate than customers using automatic payment methods.

5. Kaplan-Meier Survival Curve

The Kaplan-Meier analysis shows that customer survival probability decreases as tenure increases. The estimated median survival time is about 34 months.

6. Log-rank Test

The gender groups do not show a statistically significant survival difference. However, OnlineSecurity shows a strong survival difference. Customers with OnlineSecurity have higher survival probability and lower churn risk.

7. Cox Model and AFT Model

The Cox model suggests that OnlineBackup and TechSupport are associated with lower churn risk, while Fiber optic internet service is associated with higher churn risk. The AFT model provides another perspective by directly modeling customer retention time.

8. Customer Lifetime Value

Survival probabilities can be used to calculate customer lifetime value. In this project, the 60-month cumulative NPV is estimated as 982.10 under the given assumptions.

9. Conclusion

Survival analysis is more informative than a simple churn classification task because it answers not only whether a customer may churn, but also when the churn may happen. In this case, service features, payment method, and internet service type are closely related to customer retention.
